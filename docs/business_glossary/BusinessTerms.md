## Customer-Related Terms

### Customer
**Definition:** An individual or entity that has registered with the organization and may make purchases.

**Business Rules:**
- Identified by unique `customer_id` from source CRM system
- Must have registration date
- Tracked with full history of address changes (SCD Type 2)

**Owner:** Customer Success Team  
**Source System:** CRM

---

### Customer Segment
**Definition:** Classification of customers based on value or behavior patterns.

**Valid Values:**
- **VIP:** High-value customers (lifetime value > $10,000)
- **Premium:** Mid-high value customers
- **Standard:** Regular customers
- **Basic:** Entry-level customers

**Business Rules:**
- Assigned at registration
- Can be updated based on behavior
- Used for targeted marketing campaigns

**Owner:** Marketing Team  
**Source System:** CRM

---

### Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
**Definition:** Total revenue generated by a customer since registration.

**Formula:** `SUM(net_amount)` from all transactions for the customer

**Business Rules:**
- Calculated and stored in `dim_customer.total_spent`
- Updated after each ETL run
- Excludes returns and refunds

**Usage:**
- Customer segmentation
- Marketing campaign targeting
- Customer retention strategies

**Owner:** Analytics Team  
**Refresh Frequency:** Daily

---

### Age Group
**Definition:** Customer age segmentation for demographic analysis.

**Categories:**
- 18-24: Young Adults
- 25-34: Young Professionals
- 35-44: Mid-Career
- 45-54: Established Professionals
- 55-64: Pre-Retirement
- 65+: Retirement Age

**Business Rules:**
- Derived from `date_of_birth`
- Calculated as current age, not stored age
- Used for marketing segmentation

**Owner:** Marketing Team

---

### Churn Risk
**Definition:** Likelihood that a customer will stop making purchases.

**Calculation:** Customer with no purchases in last 180 days (6 months)

**Business Rules:**
- Triggers retention marketing campaigns
- Reviewed monthly
- Excludes customers marked as inactive

**Owner:** Customer Success Team

## Transaction & Sales Terms

### Transaction
**Definition:** A single purchase event containing one or more product line items.

**Identifiers:**
- `transaction_id`: System-generated unique ID
- `order_number`: Customer-facing order reference

**Business Rules:**
- Must have valid customer
- Must have at least one line item
- Includes date/time, payment method, and totals

**Owner:** Sales Operations  
**Source System:** E-Commerce Platform

---

### Transaction Line Item
**Definition:** Individual product purchase within a transaction.

**Grain:** One row per product per transaction

**Business Rules:**
- Must reference valid transaction_id
- Must reference valid product_id
- Quantity must be positive
- Price captured at time of sale (may differ from current price)

**Owner:** Sales Operations

---

### Net Amount
**Definition:** Final revenue for a transaction line item after discounts.

**Formula:** `line_total - discount_amount`

**Business Rules:**
- Used for all revenue calculations
- Always in USD
- Excludes tax and shipping (separate fields)

**Usage:**
- Revenue reporting
- Commission calculations
- Profitability analysis

**Owner:** Finance Team

---

### Average Order Value (AOV)
**Definition:** Average revenue per transaction.

**Formula:** `SUM(net_amount) / COUNT(DISTINCT transaction_id)`

**Business Rules:**
- Calculated at transaction level (all line items combined)
- Excludes cancelled orders
- Typically reported monthly

**Usage:**
- Sales performance tracking
- Marketing campaign effectiveness
- Pricing strategy

**Owner:** Sales Team

---

## Product Terms

### Product
**Definition:** An item available for purchase through the e-commerce platform.

**Attributes:**
- Unique product_id
- Name, category, subcategory
- Brand
- Pricing (unit cost, retail price)

**Business Rules:**
- Must belong to valid category
- Must have non-zero retail price
- Status can be Active or Inactive

**Owner:** Product Management  
**Source System:** E-Commerce Platform

---

### Product Category
**Definition:** High-level product classification.

**Valid Values:**
- Electronics
- Clothing
- Home & Garden
- Sports & Outdoors
- Books & Media

**Business Rules:**
- Required for all products
- Used for inventory management
- Drives merchandising strategies

**Owner:** Product Management

---

### Margin Percentage
**Definition:** Profit margin on a product.

**Formula:** `((retail_price - unit_cost) / retail_price) * 100`

**Business Rules:**
- Calculated and stored in dim_product
- Updated with price changes
- Target margins vary by category

**Usage:**
- Pricing decisions
- Promotion planning
- Profitability analysis

**Owner:** Finance Team

---

### Best Seller
**Definition:** Product ranking based on sales volume.

**Calculation:** Top 20 products by `SUM(quantity)` in reporting period

**Business Rules:**
- Typically reported monthly or quarterly
- Excludes promotional/clearance items
- Used for inventory planning

**Owner:** Sales Team

---

## Marketing Terms

### Campaign
**Definition:** Marketing initiative designed to promote products or services.

**Attributes:**
- Campaign name
- Start/end dates
- Budget
- Channel (Email, Social, Display, Search)
- Target customer segment

**Business Rules:**
- Must have defined date range
- Budget tracked against actual spend
- Performance measured by ROI

**Owner:** Marketing Team  
**Source System:** Marketing Automation Platform

---

### Campaign Response
**Definition:** Customer interaction with a marketing campaign.

**Types:**
- **Opened:** Customer viewed the message
- **Clicked:** Customer clicked through
- **Converted:** Customer made a purchase

**Business Rules:**
- Linked to specific campaign and customer
- Tracks conversion value
- Used for ROI calculation

**Owner:** Marketing Team

---

### Campaign ROI
**Definition:** Return on investment for a marketing campaign.

**Formula:** `((Total Conversion Value - Campaign Cost) / Campaign Cost) * 100`

**Business Rules:**
- Positive ROI indicates profitable campaign
- Target ROI varies by channel
- Reviewed monthly

**Usage:**
- Campaign effectiveness evaluation
- Budget allocation decisions
- Channel optimization

**Owner:** Marketing Team

---

### Conversion Rate
**Definition:** Percentage of campaign recipients who make a purchase.

**Formula:** `(Conversions / Total Recipients) * 100`

**Business Rules:**
- Varies significantly by channel
- Email typically 2-5%
- Paid search typically 5-10%

**Usage:**
- Campaign performance measurement
- A/B testing
- Channel effectiveness

**Owner:** Marketing Team

---

### Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)
**Definition:** Average cost to acquire one customer through a campaign.

**Formula:** `Campaign Budget / Number of Conversions`

**Business Rules:**
- Lower CPA is better
- Compared against customer lifetime value
- Target CPA varies by customer segment

**Usage:**
- Campaign profitability
- Budget optimization
- Channel selection

**Owner:** Marketing Team

## Data Quality Terms

### Data Quality Score
**Definition:** Overall measure of data fitness for use, expressed as percentage.

**Calculation:** Average of all dimension scores (Completeness, Accuracy, Consistency, Validity, Uniqueness, Timeliness, Integrity)

**Thresholds:**
- **Excellent:** 95-100%
- **Good:** 80-94%
- **Fair:** 60-79%
- **Poor:** <60%

**Business Rules:**
- Calculated daily after ETL
- Alerts triggered if below 80%
- Executive dashboard metric

**Owner:** Data Engineering Team

---

### Completeness
**Definition:** Percentage of required fields that are populated with non-null values.

**Formula:** `(Non-null required fields / Total required fields) * 100`

**Business Rules:**
- Critical fields must have >95% completeness
- Warning if 80-95%
- Failure if <80%

**Owner:** Data Engineering Team

---

### Accuracy
**Definition:** Degree to which data correctly represents real-world values.

**Validation:**
- Range checks (e.g., age 18-120)
- Format validation (e.g., email format)
- Business rule compliance

**Owner:** Data Engineering Team

---

### SCD Type 1
**Definition:** Slowly Changing Dimension strategy where changes overwrite existing values (no history).

**Use Cases:**
- Customer contact information (email, phone)
- Product descriptions
- Minor corrections

**Business Rules:**
- Latest value always used
- No historical tracking
- Simple and space-efficient

**Owner:** Data Engineering Team

---

### SCD Type 2
**Definition:** Slowly Changing Dimension strategy that maintains full history of changes.

**Implementation:**
- New row created for each change
- `valid_from` and `valid_to` date columns
- `is_current` flag for active record

**Use Cases:**
- Customer address changes
- Enables historical analysis
- Required for accurate reporting

**Owner:** Data Engineering Team

## Technical Terms

### Dimension Table
**Definition:** Reference/lookup table containing descriptive attributes about business entities.

**Characteristics:**
- Relatively small (hundreds to thousands of rows)
- Slowly changing
- Contains business keys and surrogate keys
- Provides context for fact data

**Examples:**
- dim_customer
- dim_product
- dim_date
- dim_campaign

**Owner:** Data Engineering Team

---

### Fact Table
**Definition:** Central table containing quantitative measures and foreign keys to dimensions.

**Characteristics:**
- Large (millions to billions of rows)
- Contains metrics/measures
- Grain defined at lowest level of detail
- Optimized for aggregation

**Examples:**
- fact_transactions
- fact_campaign_responses

**Owner:** Data Engineering Team

---

### Surrogate Key
**Definition:** System-generated unique identifier (typically integer) used as primary key in dimensional model.

**Characteristics:**
- Meaningless integer (no business meaning)
- Auto-incrementing
- Never changes
- Enables SCD Type 2 implementation

**Examples:**
- customer_key
- product_key
- campaign_key

**Owner:** Data Engineering Team

---

### Grain
**Definition:** Level of detail represented by a single row in a fact table.

**Example:** 
- Fact_transactions grain: "One row per product per transaction"
- Each product purchased creates one row

**Business Rules:**
- Must be clearly defined
- Cannot mix grains in same table
- All dimensions must align with grain

**Owner:** Data Engineering Team

---

### Degenerate Dimension
**Definition:** Dimension key that is stored in fact table (not separate dimension table).

**Characteristics:**
- High cardinality
- No associated descriptive attributes
- Often transactional identifiers

**Examples:**
- transaction_id
- order_number
- invoice_number

**Owner:** Data Engineering Team

---

### ETL (Extract, Transform, Load)
**Definition:** Process of extracting data from sources, transforming it according to business rules, and loading into target warehouse.

**Phases:**
1. **Extract:** Read from source systems (CSV, databases)
2. **Transform:** Apply business logic, cleansing, SCD processing
3. **Load:** Insert/update target tables

**Business Rules:**
- Runs daily at scheduled time
- Includes data quality validation
- Logged and monitored
- Can be re-run if needed

**Owner:** Data Engineering Team
